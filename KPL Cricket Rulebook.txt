KRATU PREMIER LEAGUE (KPL)
Official Rulebook
Organised by Kratu Hall of Residence, VSSUT Burla
Franchise Owners and Team Formation Rules
1. Franchise Structure
1. The tournament will consist of 4 franchises (teams).
2. Each franchise will have two owners:
   * 1 Playing Owner
   * 1 Non-Playing Owner
3. The playing owner may participate as a player in matches.
4. The non-playing owner will assist in team strategy, management, and player selection.
2. Team Formation
1. Apart from the playing owner, all players must be acquired through the auction process.
2. Each team must build its squad through auction within the defined purse limit.
3. Squad Size
1. Minimum players in a squad: 13
2. Maximum players in a squad: 16
4. Domestic and Foreign Players
1. Foreign players are defined as players from hostels other than Kratu Hall of Residence.
2. Each team can purchase a maximum of 5 foreign players in the squad.
5. Playing XI Restrictions
1. Each team can play 11 players in a match.
2. Among the 11 players:
   * Minimum 8 must be domestic players.
   * Maximum 3 foreign players are allowed.
6. Owner Responsibilities
1. Franchise owners are responsible for:
   * Budget management
   * Player selection
   * Team discipline
2. Owners must ensure compliance with all auction and match rules.
7. Arrival Rules
1. Teams must arrive at the ground at least 15 minutes before the scheduled match time.
2. If a team fails to report within this time:
   * The opposing team will be declared the winner by walkover.
Auction Rules and Regulations
1. Auction Administration
1. The Auction Master for KPL will be Sourav Padhy.
2. The decision of the Auction Master is final and binding.
2. Registration and Purse
1. Each team pays a registration fee of ₹1500.
2. Each franchise receives a virtual purse of 125 crore for the auction (IPL-style system).
3. Player Categories
1. Players will be displayed with:
   * Name
   * Photo
   * Player role (Batsman / Bowler / All-rounder)
2. Some players are classified as Premium Players.
4. Base Prices
1. Premium Players base price: 2 crore
2. Other players base price: 50 lakh
3. If a premium player remains unsold, their base price remains 2 crore.
5. Auction Method
1. Players will be selected for auction through a chit system.
2. Teams place bids until the highest bid wins the player.
6. Auction Rounds
1. If a player remains unsold:
   * They will be reintroduced in 2 later auction rounds.
2. After three rounds, if still unsold:
   * The player enters the unsold players list.
7. Post-Auction Purchases
1. Teams may purchase unsold players before matches begin given that they have less than 16 players & money left in their purse.
2. If two teams satisfying the above condition want the same player:
   * A mini auction will be conducted in front of the Auction Master from the remaining purse value.
8. Mandatory Auction Participation
1. Each team must select at least 2 players during the auction.
9. Budget Responsibility
1. Franchise owners must ensure:
   * Proper budget allocation
   * Minimum squad size compliance.
Match Rules and Tournament Format
1. Tournament Format
1. The tournament will consist of 4 teams.
2. Each team will play every other team twice.
3. Matches will be held at the ATRi Ground, VSSUT Burla.
2. Match Overs
1. Match overs will be decided by mutual agreement between both franchise owners depending on timing constraints.
2. If there is disagreement:
   * The match will default to 6 overs per side.
3. Possible agreed formats include 5, 6, 7, or 8 overs.
3. Final Match
1. The final match will be 10 overs per side.
2. This rule cannot be modified.
4. Playing XI Rules
1. Each team will field 11 players.
2. Maximum 3 foreign players can play in a match.
3. If more than 3 foreign players are used:
   * The team forfeits the match.
5. Ball Rules
1. A new sixit ball will be used at the start of every match.
2. If a batsman hits a six and the ball is lost:
   * A new ball will be issued.
6. Walkover Rule
1. If a team fails to arrive 15 minutes before the scheduled match time:
   * The match will be awarded to the opponent.
7. Umpire Selection
Each match will be officiated by umpires mutually agreed upon by the two participating teams. Each team may nominate its preferred umpires, and the umpiring duties will be carried out on a rotational basis between them, as commonly practiced in informal matches. This arrangement is intended to ensure fairness and prevent any partiality toward either team.
8. Prize Money (Tentative) 
1. Winner: Up to ₹8000
2. Runner-up: Up to ₹5000
3. Final prize distribution will be announced later by organizers depending on sponsorship amount.
